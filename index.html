Redesigning MOBA

The Museum of Bad Art (MOBA) is a non-profit devoted to the upliftment of art that "would not be displayed and appreciated in any other forum". In this redesign, I address the Join page, which allows users to manage their newsletter subscription status and donate to MOBA. Unfortunately, for such an important page, it features a confusing layout and poor contrast that makes it difficult to process the information onscreen. Thus, with usability metrics in-hand, I set out to create a new and improved version.

Disclaimer

Critics may view this redesign as hypercritical - i.e. what's the point in redesigning a website for bad art? What if a shabby design is exactly what they're looking for - a form of bad art in itself? To that I respond: I am no art critic, but any website, no matter how artsy, can be assessed in terms of usability. To enjoy and explore their exhibitions, users must be able to glean useful information from their website. For that end, good design is irreplaceable.

Problems with Current Interface

The current webpage features a host of usability issues, which may be broken down into the following categories.

Learnability

  • No clear text hierarchy through colors or size. Titles are all similar sizes, some are colored light-green for no apparent reason.
  • Hard to navigate and gain an overview of the page - too much text on screen
  • Forms are duplicated on the main page and the footer, leaving users to wonder what the difference between them is
Text is squashed No heirarchy or table of contents

Memorability

  • Green headings have poor contrast, making them hard to read and easy to forget. Same goes for the yellow text
  • Text hierarchy is unstructured and confusing. Hard to know where to look
  • Text spans the full screen, making it hard to focus on
Text has poor contrast Making it easy to overlook and forget

Efficiency

  • User has to scroll to find the forms and donate button at the bottom of the page.
  • The mobile version is cut in half (leaving half the available space blank and causing text to bleed out of containers), making it uncomfortable and inefficient to read
Mobile layout is cut in half Hard and uncomfortable to read

Low Fidelity Prototypes

How these wireframes fix usability issues

  • Learnability: Table of contents on the side allows for easy navigation, giving structure to the text on screen. Header is much larger, making it easier to distinguish from body text.
  • Memorability: Higher contrast font color makes text easier to read and remember. Text no longer spans the full screen width, making it easier to focus on.
  • Efficiency: Table of contents allows for speedy navigation; no more endless scrolling and searching.
